The Blackthorn family does not, precisely speaking, have a particularly high reputation, and the latest scion of that name, Friar Gregor Blackthorn, Knight Chaplain of the order of (X) is no exception.

It's said that madness and glory haunt the family; The Blackthorn family has always flirted with the divine and the arcane, however Gregor's sire five generations prior, was the real thing; a capable and renowned priest gifted with a knowledge cleric's psychometry. His offspring not so much. 

In the shadow of a legend, his descendants pursued knowledge and power with devout, albeit often misguided fervor. Their zeal for surpassing their patriarchs renown often led them down paths darkened by ambition and vanity. Accusations (rightly laid) of charlatanism, heresy (not entirely inaccurately accused) and even dark consortations (It was a one time thing!) were not uncommon among the whispers spoken of the family.

Devout? Certainly. Voracious seekers of knowledge? Undoubtedly. Capable.. sometimes. Honest? Quite rarely. Sane? Well, that's in the seven eyes of the beholder.

Gregor channels this familial streak for exploration of the mystic into the monstrous. His decision to pledge a knightly order initially seemed a misstep, given his physical frailties. Yet, stubbornness (coupled with a handful of arcane secrets) and frankly, sheer hateful spite carved his path as a fearsome enforcer of divine law and retribution. Armed with a laughably mundane walking stick, he normally strikes an unimposing figure, at least until he connects.

Gregor's journey at first took a common track of priories and wandering parishes, hunting monstrosities with his brethren, at least until curiosity led him to an eldritch tome after the destruction of a particularly unpleasant creature. Rather than burn the tome as one wisely ought, he instead tried to claim the knowledge for his own.

He burned the book shortly afterwards; but forgetting is another tale.
