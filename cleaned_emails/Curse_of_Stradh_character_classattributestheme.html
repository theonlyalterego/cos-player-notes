My DM Assistant suggests these:

In Dungeons & Dragons 5e, a few builds can effectively utilize thrown weapons, although they may not match the sustained ranged power of an archer-style character (like a Ranger). However, some classes and subclasses can be optimized for thrown weapons with a bit of tweaking. Here are some options:

### 1. **Fighter (Battle Master or Champion)**
   - **Battle Master**: This subclass offers great flexibility for a thrown-weapon build. The *Precision Attack* maneuver can help you overcome the lower attack bonuses associated with thrown weapons, while *Quick Toss* (from *Tasha's Cauldron of Everything*) lets you make a thrown weapon attack as a bonus action. *Sharpshooter* feat can also help increase damage output.
   - **Champion**: The *Improved Critical* feature is useful for maximizing damage over time with frequent attacks.

### 2. **Rogue (Thief or Swashbuckler)**
   - **Thief**: This subclass can be quite effective, especially with the *Fast Hands* feature, which can allow more thrown attacks or utility on the battlefield.
   - **Swashbuckler**: The Swashbuckler's *Fancy Footwork* lets you get in close, throw a dagger, and disengage without opportunity attacks. A combination of sneak attack damage with thrown weapons like daggers or handaxes can work, though it requires setting up advantageous positions.

### 3. **Barbarian (Path of the Berserker or Path of the Beast)**
   - **Berserker**: The *Frenzy* feature lets you make extra attacks with thrown weapons when raging, though the downside of exhaustion is something to watch out for.
   - **Path of the Beast**: At level 3, you gain the ability to create natural thrown weapons with the *Form of the Beast* feature. This adds flexibility and flavor, and the weapons return to your hand after being thrown.

### 4. **Ranger (Hunter or Gloom Stalker)**
   - **Hunter**: The *Colossus Slayer* or *Horde Breaker* features add extra damage to attacks, including thrown weapon attacks.
   - **Gloom Stalker**: The Gloom Stalker’s extra attack in the first round of combat is great for burst damage, and with thrown weapons, this can give you a solid opening strike.

### 5. **Monk (Kensei)**
   - **Kensei**: With *Kensei's Shot* and the ability to make ranged attacks with monk weapons (like daggers or darts), the Kensei monk can be a highly mobile, ranged combatant who uses thrown weapons. The martial arts dice can be leveraged for high damage.

### Feats to Consider:
   - **Sharpshooter**: Normally for ranged weapons, but if your DM rules that thrown weapons count, this can help negate disadvantage from long-range throws and boost damage.
   - **Dual Wielder**: Can be used to dual-wield thrown weapons like daggers or handaxes.
   - **Tavern Brawler**: Gives proficiency with improvised weapons and lets you make thrown attacks with them.

A Fighter (Battle Master) or Ranger (Hunter) would be the closest to an archer-like playstyle with thrown weapons, but with careful build choices, you can create an effective thrown-weapon character with other classes as well.


Again, I'm open to all extra sources as long as you come up with a workable class with well defined rules.
