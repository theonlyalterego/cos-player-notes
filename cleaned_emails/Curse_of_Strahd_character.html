"X"s Harrowing Event

It was meant to be an ordinary night—a peaceful gathering in the village hall, where the few villagers who remained sat together, laughing and sharing stories. The children played by the hearth, while their parents looked on, relaxed and at ease. The air was warm with the smell of fresh bread and stew, and the crackle of the fire filled the room with comforting light.

And then the door burst open.

At first, it was the wind that rushed in, howling as it tore through the room, scattering papers and blowing out candles. But then, in the shadow of the doorway, something much worse appeared. A figure—towering, hulking—stepped into the room, its head nearly scraping the wooden beam above. Its eyes gleamed with an unnatural, predatory hunger, glowing a sickly yellow in the dim light. Moonlight from the broken door reflected off the matted fur of its massive, muscled frame.

Before anyone could scream, the werewolf lunged.

It was chaos. Screams and cries filled the air as the beast moved with terrifying speed. Claws tore through flesh and bone with sickening ease. Blood sprayed across the wooden floor, staining the walls and hearth as the creature tore through the villagers like they were nothing but prey.

You remember the feeling of helplessness—the cold, numbing shock as you watched the monster tear apart those you knew, the people who had shared meals and laughter with you just moments before. The villagers tried to run, but the werewolf hunted them down, one by one, with chilling efficiency.

But you? You stayed. You couldn’t move, couldn’t speak—paralyzed by fear, or perhaps some darker force at work. And then, amidst the carnage, the werewolf turned its glowing eyes on you.

It stalked toward you, blood dripping from its maw, its claws wet with the lives it had just taken. You could hear its heavy breathing, could smell the scent of iron and death in the air. You thought you would die.

But instead, it stopped.

The werewolf tilted its head as if curious, and then it leaned in close, its hot breath on your face. You stared into those terrible eyes—so close you could see your own reflection in them—and then… it laughed. A deep, guttural, mocking sound. And in that moment, you understood: it had chosen you. Not to die, but to live. It turned and left the room, vanishing into the night, leaving behind only the dead and the sound of the wind through the broken door.

Why had it spared you?

That question has haunted you ever since. The monster could have killed you as easily as it had the others, but it didn’t. It wanted you to live—with the weight of what you saw, the deaths you could not stop.

It was a message. A challenge. One you cannot ignore.

My next character is the origin story of Francis, Eidolon of Grisnakh the Summoner.

"Francis" is his birth name, but where he comes from, everyone just calls him "X".  He was an executioner, and a hulk of a man.  In terms of looks, he's basically Nathan Explosion from Dethklok.  He became an adventurer because he was tired of waiting for the next scheduled execution, and wanted to be his own boss.  There's also the "small" matter of dreams of <<a death god he worships>> encouraging more impressive pursuits.

His demeanor is mostly bored from the banality of life.  However, when you're doing what you love, you never work a day in your life.  His barbarian rage is less unbridled anger as it is a passionate desire.  The thrill of the hunt.  The reaper is inevitable.  SWING OF THE AXE!
