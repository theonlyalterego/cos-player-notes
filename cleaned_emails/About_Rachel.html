On Wed, Mar 20, 2024 at 9:34 PM Roland Zeuge <roland.zeuge@gmail.com> wrote:
Rachel's Ghosts:

Gerrhold:  Rachel's cousin and the first to die.  Never infected by Ghoul Fever, he was "lucky" enough to be flat out killed by the first ghoul attack while everyone else tried to get away.
Gerrhold was interested in weapons and armor, and would have tried to find a position as an apprentice blacksmith in The City.
His ghost advises Rachel on weapons and armor; he prefers shiny edged weapons and thinks that bludgeoning weapons are the mark of low-skill thugs.  He definitely prefers that Rachel use a Rapier or dagger (Main-Gauche) in combat, and will get on her case if she gets lazy about maintenance.  Weapons need to be cleaned after combat, polished, sharpened, and oiled.

Pitta:  Rachel's older sister and her closest friend.  She was infected by Ghoul Fever in the initial attack and died a few days later, then rose as a ghoul and had to be put down, to date still the most traumatic event in Rachel's life.
Pitta was a skilled seamstress and would have opened a tailor's shop in The City.  Her ghost offers (mostly disapproving) fashion advice for Rachel and others, which she demands that Rachel passes on.  Pitta is somewhat of a mother hen, making sure that Rachel eats enough, gets enough sleep, and at least tries to live a healthy life.  She's worried about Rachel's state of mind and concerned that she's not making enough friends.  She thinks Rachel should be putting more effort into finding a husband and having kids, and spending less time going on adventures and putting herself at risk.  Definitely thinks that Rachel should have a daughter, and name her Pitta.

Awjis: Not a blood relative but close enough that it never mattered.  When he was close to dying of Ghoul Fever, was put down by Facaccia. 
Pitta was constantly trying to "set up" a relationship between Awjis and Rachel, which might have eventually happened, but at the time, both were too young and awkward.  
Awjis was a skilled chef and baker, even by Halfling standards, and a pious follower of Chauntea (assuming the normal D&D pantheon?).  He thinks that Rachel's life would improve if she spent more time in church, any church, and worry less about money or trying to get along in the secular world.  His advice is usually restricted to some light tut-tutting when Rachel is about to do something stupid, and he's much more eloquent after the fact in telling her what she should have done, well after it's too late.

Facaccia: Rachel's cousin, both were born on the same day, they grew up together and looked similar enough that most people assumed that they were twin sisters.  She was infected with Ghoul Fever in the initial attack, and like every idiot in a zombie movie, hid it until she turned at the worst possible moment.  
Facaccia was a talented soprano singer, and would have joined a chorus or possibly become a performer or a bard had she survived. Her ghost is bitter about her death, and has become paranoid and suspicious.  If Rachel makes a Sense Motive or Perception check (er, I mean, "Insight"), it's possibly due to Facaccia's vigilance.  Good initiative checks are also likely thanks to a timely warning from Facaccia's ghost.  She will frequently get into arguments with Pitta about whether or not to trust some random NPC.
Facaccia also has the unfortunate ability to bring out Bad Rachel at inopportune times.  If Awjis is the angel on one shoulder, Facaccia is the devil on the other.  

Philippe: Rachel's older half-brother, called "Mister" by his friends and most of his acquaintances.  The last of the group to die.  Helped Rachel kill the ghoul which Facaccia had become.  Got bitten and infected in the process.  Begged Rachel to put him out of his misery as he was raving and dying of Ghoul Fever.  Bad Rachel did it.  Good Rachel wanted to bring him to a healer somehow.
Mister was a poet and a dreamer.  A head-in-the-clouds idealist, too pure for this cruel world.  His ghost still tries to bring out the best in Rachel, her kindness and understanding.  He's still trying to get Rachel to forgive herself for how things turned out.
His verses are the surest way to relax Bad Rachel get Good Rachel back in control.  To others, his poetry is on the melodramatic and emo side, the Lord Byron school of "She walks in beauty, like the night; Of cloudless climes and starry skies"-ish-ness, but it's very meaningful to Rachel and she'll find herself reciting it to focus and calm down in bad situations.  By game time I hope to have a small library of verses, which Rachel will recite at the end of encounters.  


You've probably guessed already, Bad Rachel is the secret evil personality that Good Rachel doesn't want anyone to know about.  But the real secret is that there is no secret, both Bad Rachel and Good Rachel are the same character, sometimes circumstances bring out the worst in her, sometimes the best.  
I'll try not to derail your game ;)

On Tue, Mar 19, 2024 at 5:24 PM Roland Zeuge <roland.zeuge@gmail.com> wrote:
Hiya,

I hope this email doesn't end up being too rambling.  I'm just summarizing the ideas I have for Rachel, what I had in mind, and how it's going to be affected by the ... unusual ... mixed bag of a background that I randomly rolled.

There's going to be more fluff here than crunch, because apparently choices don't matter much in 5e, at least not for first level, and at least not compared to PF.  Also because I don't really know the crunch much yet.  I'll make those decisions later to fit my story.

She's going to be a Halfling Rogue.  That much is set.  High Dex, decent Charisma, big mouth, willing to make herself scarce if need be.  A hot temper that can get her in trouble sometimes.  Questionable decision-making skills.  A portrait painter by trade, in good standing with the Guild ("Guild Artisan").  A vegetarian with a notorious sweet tooth.  Everything was coming together.

Then I rolled this:
harrowing event 10: You did terrible things to avenge the murder of someone you loved. You became a monster, and it haunts your waking dreams
personality trait 2: I like to read and memorize poetry. It keeps me calm and brings me fleeting moments of happiness
ideals 6: I’m a monster that destroys other monsters, and anything else that gets in my way. (Evil)
bonds 2: I would sacrifice my life and my soul to protect the innocent
flaws 6: I talk to spirits that no one else can see.
trinket 73: A wolf’s head wrought in silver that is also a whistle.

So.  That's a handful.  Clearly this poor girl has gone through some bad shit.  

How the hell do I make this work?  And how do I square that with the hippy-dippy artist chick that I was going for??

Probably with lots of background trauma.  Sometimes it sucks being a PC.

Rachel grew up in Tobville, the same farming community as her cousin Reuben, who sadly never returned from his travels and adventures.

Years later, Rachel and an assorted gaggle of siblings and cousins left Tobville to see the world.  I'll provide a list of names, because this is going to be important later, but right now I'm just brainstorming.  Just a bunch of young, impressionable, naive halflings, with no interest in cabbage farming, looking to set up lives in The City.  They never made it.

The road to The City is pretty safe, but nothing is truly safe for a defenseless band of youngsters, especially not in a D&D setting, and especially especially not when they wander off into Ravenloft.  And because this is a D&D setting, possibly Ravenloft, they were attacked.  I'm thinking by Ghouls.  
Ghouls are no problem for even mid level adventurers, but they're horrifying for pre-level 1 NPC's.  Most of the group escaped, but not unscathed, and a day or so later, the first of the group died of Ghoul Fever.  And rose as a Ghoul at midnight.  The next days were a terrifying horror movie of awfulness, one which Rachel does not like to speak of even today.  Suffice to say, she was the only survivor, and only survived because she managed to kill all the ghouls, at great personal risk, and put her infected companions out of their misery before they died of Ghoul Fever.  Man, I feel bad just writing this.
Anyway, she eventually made it to a different town or city, put her life back together, apprenticed as a portrait painter, joined the Guild, and is now a fully accredited painter in good standing.  But she still has some physical and mental scars.

Most of the time, Rachel is a genuinely good, happy, chatty, occasionally flirty young woman.  Fun to be around.  She'd share her last loaf of bread with a hungry child.  Definitely Chaotic Good, like any proper Halfling PC.  Her traumatic experiences have mostly been channeled in a positive direction, in a love of life and the living.
Sometimes, however, "Bad Rachel" comes out.  This isn't a multiple personality thing, definitely not Jekyll and Hyde, but when stressed or threatened, or when the monsters come out of hiding. Bad Rachel is there to make sure everything turns out all right.

"You did terrible things to avenge the murder of someone you loved. You became a monster, and it haunts your waking dreams"
Bad Rachel is a survivor.  If she needs to be ruthless to survive, like killing companions who are dying of Ghoul Fever, she does what needs to be done.  
Guilt and remorse are for Good Rachel to worry about later.  And oh boy is there a lot of guilt and remorse.  Could they have been saved?  Ghoul Fever is a terrible way to die.

"I like to read and memorize poetry. It keeps me calm and brings me fleeting moments of happiness"
Specifically, poetry helps Bad Rachel to calm down and lets Good Rachel take over.  One of her original companions was a poet, she still keeps a booklet of his works, which she'll eventually get around to publishing, but for now she just listens to all that she can.

"I’m a monster that destroys other monsters, and anything else that gets in my way. (Evil)"
Rachel is not evil.  Even Bad Rachel isn't evil!
But the text is exactly and literally accurate.  Bad Rachel is a monster.  Bad Rachel understands that when monsters are involved, you hunt or be hunted.  Kill or be killed.  
I am not telling the other players about Bad Rachel.  They'll meet Good Rachel first.  And hopefully be shocked and stunned when she casually and calmly murders some monster.

"I would sacrifice my life and my soul to protect the innocent"
See?  Definitely not evil!
There is no contradiction here.  Rachel's experiences have left her with a somewhat black-and-white view of the world.  Innocent People and Monsters.
People can be shitty to each other, although Rachel doesn't choose to be.  Greedy people steal from and cheat each other.  But they don't go around killing each other.  Unless they're monsters.
Good Rachel is an Innocent.  Bad Rachel is a monster, who will kill without mercy to protect Rachel, her friends, and other innocents.
This is going to be a strange game, for sure.  

"I talk to spirits that no one else can see."
The spirits are the ghosts of her family and companions.  They give her helpful advice and insight on different topics, calling her attention to things she may have overlooked, or sometimes just being supportive and understanding.  Rachel knows that they're real.  (Only you as the DM know if they're actually real or not or if Rachel's just crazier than we thought).
Rachel is well aware that talking to ghosts which nobody else can see, and hearing voices, doesn't really come across well in polite company.  She tries to keep it hidden, and laughs it off when she gets caught as ha-ha, just talking to herself.

"A wolf’s head wrought in silver that is also a whistle"
This was a gift from her parents before she left the farm.  They promised it would bring her good luck.  She believes this is why she survived when nobody else did.
Blowing the whistle sounds an alarm, and is nearly guaranteed to bring on Bad Rachel.

I was originally thinking of going with the "Swashbuckler" Archetype, but I'm leaning more and more now toward "Inquisitive".

The road to The City is pretty safe, but nothing is truly safe for a defenseless band of youngsters, especially not in a D&D setting, and especially especially not when they wander off into Ravenloft.  And because this is a D&D setting, possibly Ravenloft, they were attacked.  I'm thinking by Ghouls.  
Ghouls are no problem for even mid level adventurers, but they're horrifying for pre-level 1 NPC's.  Most of the group escaped, but not unscathed, and a day or so later, the first of the group died of Ghoul Fever.  And rose as a Ghoul at midnight.  The next days were a terrifying horror movie of awfulness, one which Rachel does not like to speak of even today.  Suffice to say, she was the only survivor, and only survived because she managed to kill all the ghouls, at great personal risk, and put her infected companions out of their misery before they died of Ghoul Fever.  Man, I feel bad just writing this.
Anyway, she eventually made it to a different town or city, put her life back together, apprenticed as a portrait painter, joined the Guild, and is now a fully accredited painter in good standing.  But she still has some physical and mental scars.

Most of the time, Rachel is a genuinely good, happy, chatty, occasionally flirty young woman.  Fun to be around.  She'd share her last loaf of bread with a hungry child.  Definitely Chaotic Good, like any proper Halfling PC.  Her traumatic experiences have mostly been channeled in a positive direction, in a love of life and the living.
Sometimes, however, "Bad Rachel" comes out.  This isn't a multiple personality thing, definitely not Jekyll and Hyde, but when stressed or threatened, or when the monsters come out of hiding. Bad Rachel is there to make sure everything turns out all right.

"You did terrible things to avenge the murder of someone you loved. You became a monster, and it haunts your waking dreams"
Bad Rachel is a survivor.  If she needs to be ruthless to survive, like killing companions who are dying of Ghoul Fever, she does what needs to be done.  
Guilt and remorse are for Good Rachel to worry about later.  And oh boy is there a lot of guilt and remorse.  Could they have been saved?  Ghoul Fever is a terrible way to die.

"I like to read and memorize poetry. It keeps me calm and brings me fleeting moments of happiness"
Specifically, poetry helps Bad Rachel to calm down and lets Good Rachel take over.  One of her original companions was a poet, she still keeps a booklet of his works, which she'll eventually get around to publishing, but for now she just listens to all that she can.

"I’m a monster that destroys other monsters, and anything else that gets in my way. (Evil)"
Rachel is not evil.  Even Bad Rachel isn't evil!
But the text is exactly and literally accurate.  Bad Rachel is a monster.  Bad Rachel understands that when monsters are involved, you hunt or be hunted.  Kill or be killed.  
I am not telling the other players about Bad Rachel.  They'll meet Good Rachel first.  And hopefully be shocked and stunned when she casually and calmly murders some monster.

"I would sacrifice my life and my soul to protect the innocent"
See?  Definitely not evil!
There is no contradiction here.  Rachel's experiences have left her with a somewhat black-and-white view of the world.  Innocent People and Monsters.
People can be shitty to each other, although Rachel doesn't choose to be.  Greedy people steal from and cheat each other.  But they don't go around killing each other.  Unless they're monsters.
Good Rachel is an Innocent.  Bad Rachel is a monster, who will kill without mercy to protect Rachel, her friends, and other innocents.
This is going to be a strange game, for sure.  

"I talk to spirits that no one else can see."
The spirits are the ghosts of her family and companions.  They give her helpful advice and insight on different topics, calling her attention to things she may have overlooked, or sometimes just being supportive and understanding.  Rachel knows that they're real.  (Only you as the DM know if they're actually real or not or if Rachel's just crazier than we thought).
Rachel is well aware that talking to ghosts which nobody else can see, and hearing voices, doesn't really come across well in polite company.  She tries to keep it hidden, and laughs it off when she gets caught as ha-ha, just talking to herself.

"A wolf’s head wrought in silver that is also a whistle"
This was a gift from her parents before she left the farm.  They promised it would bring her good luck.  She believes this is why she survived when nobody else did.
Blowing the whistle sounds an alarm, and is nearly guaranteed to bring on Bad Rachel.

I was originally thinking of going with the "Swashbuckler" Archetype, but I'm leaning more and more now toward "Inquisitive".

Rachel's Ghosts:

Gerrhold:  Rachel's cousin and the first to die.  Never infected by Ghoul Fever, he was "lucky" enough to be flat out killed by the first ghoul attack while everyone else tried to get away.
Gerrhold was interested in weapons and armor, and would have tried to find a position as an apprentice blacksmith in The City.
His ghost advises Rachel on weapons and armor; he prefers shiny edged weapons and thinks that bludgeoning weapons are the mark of low-skill thugs.  He definitely prefers that Rachel use a Rapier or dagger (Main-Gauche) in combat, and will get on her case if she gets lazy about maintenance.  Weapons need to be cleaned after combat, polished, sharpened, and oiled.

Pitta:  Rachel's older sister and her closest friend.  She was infected by Ghoul Fever in the initial attack and died a few days later, then rose as a ghoul and had to be put down, to date still the most traumatic event in Rachel's life.
Pitta was a skilled seamstress and would have opened a tailor's shop in The City.  Her ghost offers (mostly disapproving) fashion advice for Rachel and others, which she demands that Rachel passes on.  Pitta is somewhat of a mother hen, making sure that Rachel eats enough, gets enough sleep, and at least tries to live a healthy life.  She's worried about Rachel's state of mind and concerned that she's not making enough friends.  She thinks Rachel should be putting more effort into finding a husband and having kids, and spending less time going on adventures and putting herself at risk.  Definitely thinks that Rachel should have a daughter, and name her Pitta.

Awjis: Not a blood relative but close enough that it never mattered.  When he was close to dying of Ghoul Fever, was put down by Facaccia. 
Pitta was constantly trying to "set up" a relationship between Awjis and Rachel, which might have eventually happened, but at the time, both were too young and awkward.  
Awjis was a skilled chef and baker, even by Halfling standards, and a pious follower of Chauntea (assuming the normal D&D pantheon?).  He thinks that Rachel's life would improve if she spent more time in church, any church, and worry less about money or trying to get along in the secular world.  His advice is usually restricted to some light tut-tutting when Rachel is about to do something stupid, and he's much more eloquent after the fact in telling her what she should have done, well after it's too late.

Facaccia: Rachel's cousin, both were born on the same day, they grew up together and looked similar enough that most people assumed that they were twin sisters.  She was infected with Ghoul Fever in the initial attack, and like every idiot in a zombie movie, hid it until she turned at the worst possible moment.  
Facaccia was a talented soprano singer, and would have joined a chorus or possibly become a performer or a bard had she survived. Her ghost is bitter about her death, and has become paranoid and suspicious.  If Rachel makes a Sense Motive or Perception check (er, I mean, "Insight"), it's possibly due to Facaccia's vigilance.  Good initiative checks are also likely thanks to a timely warning from Facaccia's ghost.  She will frequently get into arguments with Pitta about whether or not to trust some random NPC.
Facaccia also has the unfortunate ability to bring out Bad Rachel at inopportune times.  If Awjis is the angel on one shoulder, Facaccia is the devil on the other.  

Philippe: Rachel's older half-brother, called "Mister" by his friends and most of his acquaintances.  The last of the group to die.  Helped Rachel kill the ghoul which Facaccia had become.  Got bitten and infected in the process.  Begged Rachel to put him out of his misery as he was raving and dying of Ghoul Fever.  Bad Rachel did it.  Good Rachel wanted to bring him to a healer somehow.
Mister was a poet and a dreamer.  A head-in-the-clouds idealist, too pure for this cruel world.  His ghost still tries to bring out the best in Rachel, her kindness and understanding.  He's still trying to get Rachel to forgive herself for how things turned out.
His verses are the surest way to relax Bad Rachel get Good Rachel back in control.  To others, his poetry is on the melodramatic and emo side, the Lord Byron school of "She walks in beauty, like the night; Of cloudless climes and starry skies"-ish-ness, but it's very meaningful to Rachel and she'll find herself reciting it to focus and calm down in bad situations.  By game time I hope to have a small library of verses, which Rachel will recite at the end of encounters.  


You've probably guessed already, Bad Rachel is the secret evil personality that Good Rachel doesn't want anyone to know about.  But the real secret is that there is no secret, both Bad Rachel and Good Rachel are the same character, sometimes circumstances bring out the worst in her, sometimes the best.  
I'll try not to derail your game ;)
